When one model is a special case of another (can be obtained by specifying values for some parameters) they can be compared using an extra Sum of Squares F-test. In this case, the model that is a special case of anothe model, is also called a of the other model. A common example is comparing the separate lines to parallel lines model (the parallel lines model is the separate lines model with the coefficients on the interaction terms are zero). The simpler model (the one with fewer parameters) is our null model. A small p-value in the extra sum of squares F-test, gives evidence against the simpler model. In , you fit both models with , then use the function to do the test,
